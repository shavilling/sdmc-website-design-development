# sdmc-website-design-development(entersarawak)

## Project Overview
Design and develop a government digital service portal serving as the digital frontline that enables contactless tracking of inbound and outbound travelers access to all 33 borders in Sarawak, displaying accurate COVID-19 news, up-to-date COVID-19 SOP and statistics. These apps played an instrumental role during the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Problem Statement
During the COVID-19 pandemic, the public faced difficulties accessing accurate, centralized, and up-to-date information regarding cross-border travel procedures, COVID-19 SOPs, statistics, and travel approvals across Sarawak’s 33 border checkpoints. Existing information sources were fragmented and frequently updated, causing confusion and inefficiencies for travelers and authorities. Therefore, a government digital service portal was designed and developed as a digital frontline to enable contactless tracking of inbound and outbound travelers while providing accessible, reliable, and real-time COVID-19 information and border management services during a critical public health period.

## Objectives
- To design and develop a user-centered government digital service portal for managing inbound and outbound traveler information across Sarawak’s 33 border checkpoints.
- To provide a centralized platform for accurate and real-time COVID-19 news, SOP updates, travel requirements, and statistics.
- To enable contactless digital tracking and monitoring to improve efficiency, accessibility, and public safety during the pandemic.
- To enhance user experience through intuitive navigation, responsive design, and accessible digital services for both the public and relevant authorities.

## Outcome
Successfully developed and deployed a government digital service portal that became a key digital frontline during the COVID-19 pandemic, supporting contactless border management and public access to critical information across Sarawak’s 33 border checkpoints. The platform improved accessibility to real-time COVID-19 updates, SOP guidelines, travel procedures, and statistics through a centralized and user-friendly digital experience. The system was widely adopted and actively utilized by the public and relevant authorities, contributing to more efficient information dissemination, streamlined travel management, and safer digital interactions during the pandemic. The project received recognition through the Malaysia Technology Excellence Award (MTEA).
